# Children's Health & Pediatrics FAQs

Common questions about children's health, growth, development, and when to seek medical care.

---

## When to See a Doctor

### When should I take my child to a doctor?

A child must be immediately taken to a health institute in case of:
- Fever
- Coughing/wheezing
- Vomiting
- Stomach ache
- Diarrhea
- Constipation
- Allergy
- Weakness or shortness as compared to peers
- Headache
- Ear pain
- Difficulty in breathing
- Heart throb
- Fainting
- Poisoning
- Accidents and injuries including burns

## Growth & Development

### How often should I monitor my baby's growth and development?

The growth of babies should be monitored starting from birth, including height, weight, head circumference, etc. The frequency of these measurements should be as follows:

| Age/Time | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| During delivery | Once |
| First 48 hours post-delivery | Once |
| Day 15 | Once |
| Day 41 | Once |
| Month 2, 3, 4 | Once a month |
| Month 6, 9, 12 | Every three months |
| 1 to 3 years old | Every six months |
| 3 to 6 years old | Once a year |

## Breastfeeding & Nutrition

### Why is breast milk important?

Breast milk offers many advantages for both the mother and the baby:

- Breast milk is the ideal nutrient for the baby
- It helps strengthen the baby's immune system
- It is easily accessible and affordable
- Breastfeeding strengthens the bond between the mother and the baby
- It helps the mother balance her body weight after delivery
- It is the ideal food for the baby's young digestive system
- It protects the baby against potential allergies that may result from solid foods

### How should I feed my baby?

- Babies should be only fed with breastmilk in the **first 6 months** of their lives
- After 6 months, breast milk should be supported with solid foods
- Mothers should continue breastfeeding in addition to giving solid food until the baby is **2 years old**

## General Health Factors

### What Are The Fundamental Factors That Affect Children's Health?

**Mother's health** is the main factor that affects a child's health:
- The mother's age, health and diet prior to pregnancy
- Prenatal care
- The time between multiple births

**Other important factors include:**
- The health condition of the other members of the family
- The socio-economic condition of the family
- Living space
- Access to safe water and safe food
- Waste and other environmental factors
- School health
- Social support

A child's health may also be affected by the lifestyle, culture, religious practices, traditions, habits and child care methods of the family.
