# Health Rights and Health-Related Laws in Turkey

## Overview

Health rights are fundamental human rights that ensure every individual can access healthcare services, maintain their wellbeing, and live with dignity. In Turkey, these rights are protected by both the constitution and specific legislation.

"Right to health means all individuals can ask the society and the state to protect their health, to offer treatment and to heal their conditions when necessary and can access all means offered by the society to continuously improve individuals' health."

Right to health and right of a healthy life, which are important components of "right to life", are among the positive status rights.

### Conditions for Health Rights

Right to be healthy, maintain well-being and to improve well-being are rights which depend on the presence of certain conditions and circumstances including:

- Accessing protective healthcare services
- A healthy environment, surrounding and accommodation
- Sufficient and balanced nutrition
- Being able to improve one's well-being
- Freedom to work and choose a job, right to produce
- A democratic society and equality in human relations
- Right of opinion, faith and right to express the same

## Patients' Rights

Patients' rights were first established in the Lisbon Declaration, which broadly describes doctor-patient relationship. The fundamental rights listed in the declaration are as follows:

1. **Right to Choose**: Patients can freely choose their doctors.

2. **Right to Independent Care**: Patients have the right to be served by doctors who can make clinical and ethical decisions freely without any external influence.

3. **Right to Informed Consent**: Patients have the right to accept or reject recommended treatment after being sufficiently informed.

4. **Right to Confidentiality**: Patients have the right to expect the doctors to respect confidentiality of private information on their medical history and private life.

5. **Right to Dignity**: Patients have the right to die with honor.

6. **Right to Spiritual Support**: Patients have the right to accept or reject spiritual consolation that involves assistance from a person of clergy as appropriate.

## Key Legislation

### Law on Social Insurance and General Health Insurance (Law No. 5510)

This law establishes the framework for social security and health insurance in Turkey, defining:
- Coverage eligibility
- Benefit packages
- Premium calculations
- Rights and obligations of insured persons

### Regulation on Patient Rights

This regulation outlines specific patient rights including:
- Right to receive information about their health condition
- Right to privacy during treatment
- Right to choose their healthcare provider
- Right to refuse treatment
- Right to access medical records
