# Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

## Overview

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning or development.

ADHD is one of the most common neurodevelopmental disorders of childhood and often lasts into adulthood.

---

## Symptoms

### Inattention
- Difficulty paying attention to details
- Problems sustaining attention in tasks or play
- Not listening when spoken to directly
- Not following through on instructions
- Difficulty organizing tasks and activities
- Avoiding tasks requiring sustained mental effort
- Frequently losing things
- Easily distracted
- Forgetful in daily activities

### Hyperactivity
- Fidgeting or squirming
- Leaving seat when remaining seated is expected
- Running or climbing in inappropriate situations
- Unable to play or engage in leisure activities quietly
- Often "on the go" or acting as if "driven by a motor"
- Talking excessively

### Impulsivity
- Blurting out answers before questions are completed
- Difficulty waiting their turn
- Interrupting or intruding on others

---

## Causes and Risk Factors

The cause(s) and risk factors for ADHD are unknown, but current research shows that genetics plays an important role.

Other possible causes and risk factors being studied include:
- Brain injury
- Exposure to environmental risks (e.g., lead) during pregnancy or at a young age
- Alcohol and tobacco use during pregnancy
- Premature delivery
- Low birth weight

---

## Treatment and Intervention

Treatment and interventions require accommodations and support to become successful academically and in personal life. Once diagnosed, parents, teachers and the child's doctor should work together to make a plan.

### Medical Treatment
- Doctors may prescribe medications to help with neurological deficits
- May suggest changes to the child's diet

### Educational Support
- Teachers may need to make accommodations
- Develop teaching strategies to target certain behaviors
- Individualized Education Program (IEP)

### Parental Involvement
- Provide access to therapists
- Communication with teachers and doctors
- Reinforce expected behaviors

### Therapy
- **Psychotherapy (talk therapy)**: Discuss how ADHD affects life and ways to manage it
- **Behavioral therapy**: Learn how to monitor and manage behavior

---

## Lifestyle Changes

Lifestyle changes that can help manage symptoms:

- Eat a healthy diet with lots of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein
- Get some exercise every day (consider sports teams like basketball, soccer, or baseball)
- Limit time spent on electronic devices
- Get plenty of sleep
- Simplify the child's room to reduce distractions and improve organization

## FAQs

### What is the cause of ADHD?

The cause(s) and risk factors for ADHD are unknown, but current research shows that genetics factors play an important role. Scientists are also studying brain injury, environmental exposure to toxins, alcohol and tobacco use during pregnancy, premature delivery, and low birth weight.

### How can we treat and intervene with people having ADHD?

- Teachers may need to make accommodations and develop teaching strategies
- Parents may need to provide access to therapists and reinforce expected behaviors
- Therapy options include psychotherapy and behavioral therapy

- Eat a healthy diet with fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein
- Get exercise every day (sports teach important social skills)
