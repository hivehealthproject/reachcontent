# Eating Disorders

## Overview

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions characterized by severe disturbances in eating behaviors and related thoughts and emotions. They affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds. Eating disorders can have serious health consequences and may be life-threatening if not treated.

Common types include anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder.

---

## Types of Eating Disorders

### Anorexia Nervosa
Characterized by:
- Extremely restricted eating
- Intense fear of gaining weight
- Distorted body image
- Relentless pursuit of thinness

### Bulimia Nervosa
Characterized by:
- Recurrent episodes of eating unusually large amounts of food
- Feeling a lack of control during binges
- Compensatory behaviors (purging, excessive exercise, fasting)

### Binge-Eating Disorder
Characterized by:
- Recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of food
- Feeling a loss of control during binges
- Experiencing shame, distress, or guilt afterward
- Not regularly using compensatory measures

---

## Symptoms and Warning Signs

- Preoccupation with weight, food, calories, and dieting
- Refusal to eat certain foods or food groups
- Discomfort eating around others
- Food rituals (excessive chewing, not allowing foods to touch)
- Skipping meals or eating tiny portions
- Extreme mood swings
- Withdrawal from friends and activities
- Evidence of binge eating or purging
- Excessive exercise
- Changes in weight
- Gastrointestinal complaints
- Feeling cold all the time

---

## Causes

Although the causes of eating disorders are not fully known, they can occur for different reasons due to:
- Genetic factors
- Psychological factors
- Social factors
- Cultural factors (wanting to look more beautiful)
- Having previously suffered from psychological disorders such as depression or anxiety
- Having a family member with an eating disorder
- Changes in adolescence

---

## Treatment

It is important to start eating disorder treatment as early as possible. Although treatment takes time, recovery is possible.

### Medical Care
- Address health consequences
- Nutritional counseling
- Weight restoration if needed

### Psychotherapy
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
- Family-based treatment (especially for adolescents)
- Interpersonal psychotherapy

### Medication
- May help with co-occurring conditions (depression, anxiety)
- Specific medications for certain eating disorders

## FAQs

### Is it possible to completely get rid of the eating disorder?

It is important to start eating disorder treatment as early as possible. Although treatment takes time, it is possible to recover. For this, it is important to see a psychiatrist and start treatment as soon as possible.

### At what age does the eating disorder begin?

Although eating disorders are classified differently in themselves, 14-19 years of age is a critical period.

### Where can support be obtained?

You need to see a psychiatrist. Look for hospitals and mental health centers in your area that specialize in eating disorder treatment.

### Why is it happening?

Although the causes of eating disorders are not fully known, they can occur for different reasons due to genetic, psychological, social and cultural factors. Factors such as having previously suffered from any psychological disorders such as depression, anxiety or having a family member, changes in adolescence, cultural reasons (wanting to look more beautiful, more handsome) can be effective.
